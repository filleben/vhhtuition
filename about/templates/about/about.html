About Me

Never did I think I would grow up to be a Maths tutor, and yet here I am working my dream job! Working with children aged between 7 and 16, who have difficulties with Maths. Be it just not understanding the subject, or due to underlining learning differences. I’ve worked with children who are dyslexic or undiagnosed dyscalculia and find these learners often have their own sense of the subject already, and just need someone who can understand their thinking and bring out their own confidence.

Verity is a star tutor! She has the skills and patience of teaching kids. My daughter looks forward to her lesson each week.

Matt, KS2 and KS3 Parent, Northants

I’m dyslexic myself and so understand some of the struggles children my be experiencing with Mathematics. I have the ability to give children a different way of understanding a topic. Rather than tell them how it’s done, I’ll ask questions, encourage discussions and let them make mistakes to learn, in a safe and friendly environment, be it online or face to face. Our sessions are often filled with laughter and discussions – all which help your child to learn. I work well with most children, provided they give their best throughout their sessions and work hard.

I’m currently studying Mathematics and it’s Learning at the Open University. Which is both interesting and challenging, and gives insights into how children learn Maths. From here I hope to gain extra qualifications that will help me to work with children with SEN on a one to one in a school environment.

What does a lesson with Verity look like?

Maths sessions are available after school, Monday to Friday, Saturday morning or during the school day – ideal for those who are home educated.

I work with your child either online, or face to face, one to one. There are benefits to both, of which I look into on my blog.

A session is generally an hour or so (sometimes we may run over by five mins or so).

My hour is split into three sections: the first five minutes are for a quick catch up. This is where I’ll ask how school/their week has been and find out if there has been anything that’s cropped up over the week they would like to have a look over.

Using blocks to explore ratio (KS2/KS3)

We then either work on whatever the tutee would like to look at, or I work from my plan. Every learner works and learns differently, so how this time is used will vary. I use a number of items to help – from Cuisenaire Rods to flash cards – or discussion and worksheets.

Using cards to explore number bonds

If we have had a productive learning time, I may ask if they would like to play a game. I generally leave 5/10 minutes at the end for a maths based game. Not every child wants to play though, some will ask for more practice! I really do leave it up to the learner to chose. I have a game for factors, number bonds and mental maths, to name a few.

Talking and laughing is encouraged through the sessions – Maths is fun, and if your child can talk me through his working out, then we can pick out where (if at all) there’s anything going astray – a difficult task for most children at first!
